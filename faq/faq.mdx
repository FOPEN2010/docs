## General

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is RondoSync?">
    RondoSync is Web3 infrastructure that lets users **deposit** into Vaults, hold or record **Shares**, and participate in structured strategies.

    Depending on the Vault structure, Vault performance may be reflected through:

    - **Share Price** movements
    - **Rewards** credited to **USD Balance**
    - A combination of both

    RondoSync does not guarantee profit, fixed returns, or capital preservation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need an account?">
    RondoSync uses wallet-based access.

    - Connect your wallet
    - No traditional email or password account may be required
    - Your wallet address may act as your platform identity
    - Certain actions may require wallet signatures

    Users are responsible for securing their own wallets, private keys, seed phrases, devices, and transaction approvals.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What assets are supported?">
    RondoSync may support stablecoin-based deposits, such as USDT, depending on the Vault and network.

    Supported assets may differ by:

    - Vault
    - Network
    - Platform availability
    - Operational status
    - Applicable rules

    Users should always review the specific Vault details before depositing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What networks are supported?">
    Network support may differ by asset, Vault, and platform feature.

    For example, some networks may support **Deposit**, while others may support **Withdraw**, or may still be under development.

    Users should always confirm:

    - Correct network
    - Correct asset
    - Correct wallet address
    - Vault-specific network support

    Sending assets on the wrong network may result in permanent loss.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Vaults and Shares

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is a Vault?">
    A Vault is a structured strategy container with its own parameters and rules.

    Each Vault may define its own:

    - Strategy profile
    - Supported assets
    - Supported networks
    - Minimum deposit amount
    - Duration or lock-up period
    - Fee structure
    - Redeem conditions
    - Reward treatment
    - Accounting rules

    Users should review each Vault before participating.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are Shares?">
    **Shares** represent a user’s participation in a specific Vault.

    Shares may be used to determine:

    - The user’s proportional participation in a Vault
    - The estimated value of the user’s Vault position
    - Redeem calculations
    - Reward allocation, where applicable

    Shares do not represent a fixed return or guaranteed profit.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is Share Price?">
    **Share Price** represents the value of one **Share** in a Vault.

    Share Price may be used for:

    - Calculating how many **Shares** a user receives when depositing
    - Estimating the value of a user’s Vault position
    - Calculating Redeem amounts when a user exits a Vault

    **Share Price** may increase or decrease depending on Vault performance, fees, settlement results, liquidity, and other Vault-specific accounting rules.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can Share Price go down?">
    Yes.

    **Share Price** may rise or fall depending on Vault performance and accounting rules.

    If **Share Price** decreases, the estimated value of the user’s Vault position may also decrease.

    Users may experience gains or losses when they redeem their Vault position.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are all Vaults the same?">
    No.

    Each Vault may have different:

    - Strategy exposure
    - Risk profile
    - Supported assets
    - Minimum deposit amount
    - Lock-up period
    - Fees
    - Redeem conditions
    - Reward treatment
    - Accounting rules

    Users should not assume that all Vaults behave in the same way.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Deposit / Stake

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What does Deposit mean?">
    **Deposit** refers to placing supported assets into a Vault.

    In the app, this action may appear as **Stake**.

    A deposit may:

    - Be initiated from the user’s connected wallet
    - Be executed through smart contracts
    - Be recorded on-chain where applicable
    - Be allocated to a specific Vault
    - Be converted into **Shares** according to the applicable **Share Price** and Vault rules

    Deposited funds become part of the selected Vault position.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is Deposit the same as Withdraw?">
    No.

    **Deposit** enters a Vault.

    **Withdraw** transfers available **USD Balance** from the platform to a connected wallet.

    Active Vault **Shares** cannot be withdrawn directly. A user may need to **Redeem** first before an amount becomes available for **Withdraw**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need to sign transactions?">
    Deposits may require wallet signatures.

    Depending on the supported flow, a user may need to approve token spending and confirm the deposit transaction.

    Users should carefully verify all transaction details before signing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Redeem & Withdraw

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is the difference between Redeem and Withdraw?">
    **Redeem** and **Withdraw** are separate processes.

    | Action | Meaning | Typical Result |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | Redeem | Exit a Vault position according to Vault rules | Redeemed amount may be credited to **USD Balance** or another app-level balance |
    | Withdraw | Transfer available app balance to a connected wallet | Supported asset is sent to the user’s wallet |

    Redeem does not necessarily send funds directly to the user’s wallet.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is Redeem?">
    **Redeem** refers to exiting all or part of a Vault position according to the applicable Vault rules.

    Redeem may be affected by:

    - **Share Price**
    - Number of **Shares** being redeemed
    - Vault performance
    - Fees
    - Penalties
    - Lock-up periods
    - Liquidity conditions
    - Settlement timing
    - Security, compliance, or operational review

    The resulting amount may be credited to **USD Balance** or another app-level balance, depending on the Vault and platform rules.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I redeem anytime?">
    It depends on the Vault.

    Some Vaults may have:

    - Lock-up periods
    - Minimum holding periods
    - Redeem windows
    - Early Redeem penalties
    - Liquidity-dependent delays
    - Manual or automated review

    Users should review the specific Vault rules before depositing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is Withdraw?">
    **Withdraw** refers to transferring available **USD Balance** from RondoSync to the user’s connected wallet.

    Withdraw may be subject to:

    - Available **USD Balance**
    - Validation
    - Approval
    - Security checks
    - Compliance review
    - Operational processing
    - Network conditions
    - Transaction limits
    - Fees

    Active Vault **Shares** cannot be withdrawn directly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I withdraw anytime?">
    Users may request a **Withdraw** for available **USD Balance**.

    However, Withdraw may be subject to:

    - Validation
    - Approval
    - Security checks
    - Compliance review
    - Platform rules
    - Liquidity availability
    - Network conditions
    - Transaction limits

    Withdraw requests may be delayed, restricted, rejected, or require additional review.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long does Withdraw take?">
    Processing time may vary depending on:

    - Network congestion
    - Selected network
    - Transaction confirmation time
    - Internal validation
    - Security checks
    - Compliance review
    - Operational approval
    - Liquidity availability
    - Wallet infrastructure availability

    Processing times are not guaranteed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a Withdraw request be rejected?">
    Yes.

    A Withdraw request may be rejected, delayed, or restricted if it fails validation, triggers risk controls, requires additional review, or violates platform rules.

    Reasons may include:

    - Insufficient available **USD Balance**
    - Incorrect or unsupported network
    - Suspicious wallet activity
    - Compliance review
    - Security concerns
    - Transaction limits
    - Operational issues
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## USD Balance

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is USD Balance?">
    **USD Balance** is an in-app balance used to track amounts that may become available for Withdraw, subject to applicable conditions.

    USD Balance may include:

    - Vault Rewards
    - Referral rewards
    - Ambassador rewards
    - Redeemed amounts
    - Other credited amounts, depending on platform rules

    USD Balance is separate from active Vault **Shares**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is USD Balance a bank deposit?">
    No.

    **USD Balance** is an internal platform accounting record.

    It is not:

    - A bank deposit
    - An insured asset
    - A guaranteed claim to a specific underlying asset
    - A guarantee of immediate withdrawal

    USD Balance may be subject to platform rules, security checks, compliance review, fees, limits, and operational processing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can USD Balance change?">
    Yes.

    USD Balance may change due to:

    - Rewards credited
    - Redeemed amounts credited
    - Referral or Ambassador rewards
    - Withdraw requests
    - Fees
    - Adjustments
    - Restrictions or reviews
    - Other platform rules

    Users should review platform records and applicable policies.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Technical

    - Settlement
