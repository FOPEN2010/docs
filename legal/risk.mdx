## Overview

Using RondoSync involves risk.

RondoSync provides access to Vault-based structured strategies with **Share**-based participation, Vault-level accounting, possible **Rewards**, **Redeem**, and **Withdraw** functionality.

Users should understand that:

- Returns are not guaranteed
- Profit is not fixed or guaranteed
- Capital loss is possible
- **Share Price** may increase or decrease
- Rewards may vary by Vault
- Redeem and Withdraw may be delayed, restricted, or rejected
- Security, compliance, and operational controls may apply

Users should review all applicable Vault details, risk disclosures, terms, and policies before participating.

Users are responsible for:

## No Guarantee of Returns

RondoSync does not guarantee:

- Profitability
- Stable returns
- Fixed Rewards
- Capital preservation
- Availability of any specific Vault
- Continued access to any strategy
- Successful Redeem or Withdraw at any specific time

Any examples, projections, illustrations, or historical references are provided for informational purposes only and do not guarantee future results.

---

## Capital Loss Risk

Users may lose part or all of the capital allocated to a Vault.

Capital loss may result from:

- Market volatility
- Strategy underperformance
- Liquidity constraints
- Fees and penalties
- Negative **Share Price** movement
- Smart contract vulnerabilities
- Counterparty or venue failure
- Operational or technical failures
- Security incidents
- Regulatory or compliance restrictions
- Other events outside RondoSync’s control

Users should only participate after understanding that capital loss is possible.

---

## Vault-Specific Risk

Each Vault may have different parameters, strategies, and conditions.

Vault-specific differences may include:

- Supported assets
- Supported networks
- Minimum deposit amount
- Lock-up periods
- Redeem conditions
- Fee structures
- Reward treatment
- Accounting rules
- Strategy exposure
- Liquidity conditions
- Risk profile

Users should not assume that all Vaults operate in the same way.

---

## Share Price Risk

Vaults may use **Share Price** to represent the value of one **Share** in a Vault.

**Share Price** may increase or decrease due to:

- Vault performance
- Asset valuation
- Market conditions
- Settlement results
- Fees
- Redeem activity
- Liquidity conditions
- Other Vault-specific accounting rules

Because **Share Price** may rise or fall, users may experience gains or losses when they redeem their Vault position.

---

## Rewards Risk

Some Vaults may credit periodic **Rewards** to the user’s **USD Balance**.

Rewards may vary depending on:

- Vault performance
- User **Shares**
- Settlement period
- Vault-specific distribution rules
- Applicable fees or adjustments
- Liquidity conditions
- Operational or compliance review

Rewards are not fixed or guaranteed.

Not all Vaults may distribute Rewards in the same way. Some Vaults may primarily reflect performance through **Share Price**, while others may also credit periodic **Rewards** to **USD Balance**.

---

## Market Risk

Market conditions may directly affect Vault performance.

Market risks include:

- Crypto asset volatility
- Stablecoin risk
- Liquidity shocks
- Price dislocations
- Funding rate changes
- Macroeconomic events
- Market-wide stress events
- Depeg events involving stablecoins or other assets

Market conditions may result in reduced returns, negative performance, or loss of capital.

---

## Strategy Risk

Vault strategies may underperform.

Strategy risks include:

- Execution inefficiency
- Incorrect assumptions
- Model or strategy failure
- Adverse market conditions
- Liquidity changes
- Volatility changes
- Changes in spreads, funding rates, or market structure
- Strategy changes over time

No strategy is guaranteed to produce profit.

---

## Liquidity Risk

Liquidity conditions may affect both performance and user access to funds.

Liquidity risks include:

- Limited liquidity in underlying markets
- Lock-up periods
- Redeem windows
- Early Redeem penalties
- Processing delays
- Liquidity-dependent restrictions
- Inability to unwind positions at expected prices
- Delayed access to **USD Balance** or withdrawable assets

Users may not be able to redeem or withdraw immediately.

---

## Redeem Risk

**Redeem** refers to exiting a Vault position according to Vault rules.

Redeem risks include:

- Redeem may not be available at all times
- Redeem may be subject to lock-up periods
- Early Redeem may be restricted or penalized
- Redeem settlement may be delayed
- Redeem value may be affected by **Share Price**
- Fees, penalties, or liquidity adjustments may reduce the final amount
- Security, compliance, or operational review may apply
- The resulting amount may be credited to **USD Balance** or another app-level balance depending on platform and Vault rules

Redeem does not necessarily send funds directly to the user’s wallet.

---

## Withdraw Risk

**Withdraw** refers to transferring available **USD Balance** from the platform to a connected wallet.

Withdraw risks include:

- Withdraw may require validation or approval
- Withdraw may be delayed
- Withdraw may be rejected or restricted
- Security checks may apply
- Compliance review may apply
- Transaction limits may apply
- Liquidity availability may affect processing
- Network congestion may delay confirmation
- Fees may reduce the final amount received
- Incorrect wallet address or network selection may result in permanent loss

Active Vault **Shares** cannot be withdrawn directly.

---

## Smart Contract Risk

On-chain interactions involve technical risk.

Smart contract risks include:

- Code vulnerabilities
- Exploits
- Unexpected contract behavior
- Integration failures
- Upgrade or deployment issues
- Oracle or dependency failures
- User signing mistakes

Blockchain transactions may be irreversible once executed.

---

## Counterparty and Venue Risk

Capital may be deployed through external venues, protocols, service providers, or infrastructure.

Counterparty and venue risks include:

- Centralized exchange failure
- Decentralized protocol failure
- Custodial or operational failure
- Liquidity venue disruption
- API or connectivity failures
- Withdrawal restrictions imposed by third-party platforms
- Insolvency, hacks, or operational failures of third parties

Failure of counterparties or venues may result in partial or total loss.

- Settlement delays

- Human error
- System bugs
- Monitoring failures

- Delays in manual review or approval
- Data or calculation errors

Operational or technical issues may affect performance, access to funds, or user experience.
